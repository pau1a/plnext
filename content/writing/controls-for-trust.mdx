---
title: "Controls for Trust"
slug: "controls-for-trust"
date: "2024-07-18"
summary: "Embedding observability hooks before shipping autonomy so operators can see whether the system is keeping its promises."
---

Trustworthy systems don't ask for blind faith. They show their work.

When we introduce autonomy, we also expose the diagnostics that prove the thing is behaving. Feedback loops, operator interrupts, and explicit degradation plans mean there's always a way to slow the system down before it outruns our judgment.
